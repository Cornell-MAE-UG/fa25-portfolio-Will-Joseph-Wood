As part of a class project we were tasked with analyzing a heat exchanger.

The device loses less heat when the difference between the hot and cold temperatures are much larger (more efficient). In my calculations, we see that the system loses a lot more energy as heat when the system has a closer value in temperature between the initial and final value. The first value technically gained heat somewhere in the system for Q/M to be positive, but this is likely error from us interacting with the system or from somewhere else. Either way, the second case was much less efficient demonstrating how systems are more efficient with larger changes in temperature. The efficiency of the system decreases when the temperatures are closer in value. This is because the change is less drastic between the two so more heat gets lost due to the surroundings because there is a lower thermodynamic emphasis to transfer heat between the two fluids and a higher likelihood to transfer heat out of the system where there is a greater temperature difference. We did not analyze the system by switching the flow rates of the fluids between the two pumps, but there would likely be a change in the efficiency and temperature of the system. Since the fluids are flowing faster, friction would likely play a larger role and would likely increase the irreversibilities present within the system and thus make the system less efficient due to the increased flow rate causing energy to leave the system in the form of work.
